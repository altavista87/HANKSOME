üéì Methodology

Model Framework: EngramHANK.jl

This analysis employs the EngramHANK model, a state-of-the-art heterogeneous agent New Keynesian (HANK) framework combining:

  • DeepSeek Engram Architecture: O(1) memory lookup for pre-computed Jacobians
  • Bilal Continuous-Time SSJ: Analytic Jacobians via Master Equation
  • O(T log T) Solver: FFT-based Toeplitz arithmetic for GE iteration

Calibration Data Sources

  • BNM (Bank Negara Malaysia): Interest rates, monetary policy data, external trade
  • DOSM (Department of Statistics Malaysia): Household income surveys, labor force data

üíº Policy Implications

1. Sectoral Risk Management

The 48% output impact from Tourism Collapse reveals critical sectoral concentration. Recommendation: Establish sector-specific stabilization funds and diversification incentives to reduce dependency on single sectors.

2. External Demand Hedging

With 25% of shocks originating externally, Malaysia needs stronger automatic stabilizers for export-oriented sectors. Recommendation: Enhance EPF flexibility during external shocks and develop export credit insurance expansion.

3. Fiscal Policy Design

GST and subsidy changes show high persistence (8 quarters) despite modest initial impact. Recommendation: Phase fiscal adjustments gradually and communicate clearly to anchor expectations.

4. Labor Market Resilience

Gig economy volatility shows the longest persistence (17 quarters) and affects consumption disproportionately (13% vs 5% output). Recommendation: Develop social protection for informal workers and portable benefits systems.

5. Monetary Policy Transmission

Fed spillovers and OPR changes show gradual transmission (6-8 quarters). Recommendation: BNM should maintain forward guidance and consider macroprudential measures for financial stability.
